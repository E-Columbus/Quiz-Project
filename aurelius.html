Rome

Not every day we witness a Roman emperor who also considered essential for his reign's success to become a philosopher, that is the case of Marcus Aurelius, having embraced stoicism against his advisor's will, he embodied the knowledge Seneca, it basically involved four virtues in everyday life wisdom, courage, temperance, and justice to achieve a true state of hapiness, or in this case, to be a successful emperor.

Stoicism also teaches the development of self-control as a means of overcoming destructive emotions, the philosophy holds that becoming a clear and unbiased thinker allows one to understand the universal reason.

In terms of defining metaphysical reality the christian influence on the emperor is astounding, in his work of "Meditations" he defined it as the following:

"Constantly regard the universe as one living being, having one substance and one soul; and observe how all things have reference to one perception, the perception of this one living being; and how all things act with one movement; and how all things are the cooperating causes of all things that exist; observe too the continuous spinning of the thread and the structure of the web"
