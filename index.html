Ancient Greece

The birthplace of western civilization, from math to logic, those are some of the defining discoveries of this initial time period. But more importantly being self-aware of the existance of consiousness and a metaphysically objective definition of reality in which entities had an external existence to our mental perceptions. Metaphysical realism would guide the world until the colonial/modern age, which only reaffirms how important it was for humanity.(470bc - 322bc)

Rome

While Greece was the spirit of western culture, Rome was the body responsible for carrying it to civilize the world. The eternal city, its very name immediatly conjures images of steely eyed legionares wearing centurion helmets in our minds even after 2500 years.

Being the cradle of western civilization and prosperity responsible for our western culture, of course Rome contributed to metaphysical philosophy in many ways, its transition from the Greek pantheon of gods to Christianity as well as nurturing society with exemplary moral values even today are first-class standards. Its fall is still considered one of the great tragedies of History that sparked the beginning of the barbaric Dark Ages (753bc - 476ad)

Middle Ages

Medieval knights mounting armored horses, christian crusades and princesses living in majestic castles serving their king. After the tragic fall of Rome it would take years to assimilate the advances that had been lost in the repeated lootings of the eternal city. Without a centralized authority most feudal lords would lock themselves up in their own kingdoms and merely protect those around them and serfs who would pay them tribute in exchange for protection. For those unfortunate who lived outside city walls their property was all up for grabs since the same savage germanic tribes that had been responsible for destroying Rome were still roaming free, however even when locked up in their monasteries, medieval monks were still able to put the pieces of the puzzle back together and revive the long lost metaphysical realism stronger than ever before (476ad - 1492ad)

Colonial Age

God replaced by kings, then kings replaced by a burgeois class, then those same wealthy merchants executed by the population, a period of neverending volition without ever reaching the stability of medieval times or the pax romana, events like the French revolution still evoke an immediate feeling of instability for example, but why exactly this instability happened in the first place?.

The last time period, also called Modern Age, basically lasts from the discovery of the Americas up until the beginning of World War I. At its dawn we can find the most prominent reason responsible for today's attitudes towards life of individuals: The protestant reformation, its unforseen consecuences even for the authors behind it at the time gave birth to metaphysical relativism, nihilism, hedonism and a myriad of morally questionable decisions by lots of modern day authors in hindsight.

But if there's one thing we learned in this journey so far is that metaphysical philosophy is the core reason that much later triggers these historical events. (1492ad - 1914ad)
